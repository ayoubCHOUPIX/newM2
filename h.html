Th greatest football legends

PELE

There’s always debate about who’s the greatest football player of all time. Pele was the first to be unanimously awarded that title and those who’ve seen him play call him the finest ever. Despite never playing for a European club, Pele’s legacy remains as strong as ever. For all of the modern football greats, he is the ghost to chase as the dreams of emulating him reverberate in every football player on the planet.

DIEGO MARADONA

The last time Argentina won the World Cup was in 1986 and the man who led them to the title was ‘El Magico’, Diego Armando Maradona. Widely notorious for his short-temper but also worshipped by many for his amazing talent, Maradona was the ultimate maverick. He could dribble past players like a hot knife through butter. He could pick out a teammate with a fantastic pass that would leave defenses stunned and he could produce moments of magic on a daily basis.

JOHAN CRUYFF

Born in Amsterdam, Cruyff was the crown jewel of football in the Netherlands and was the catalyst in the European nation’s rise to football royalty. When Rinus Michels developed his game of totaalvoetball, Cruyff was the deep-lying forward who made the formation and strategy tick.
